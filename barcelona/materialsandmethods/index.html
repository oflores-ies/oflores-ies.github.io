Materials and methods

To make this project happen, we used R and Python to study air pollution. We started by cleaning up the data, getting rid of messy parts and "junk" info we didn't need. We also made sure the dates and times were perfectly organized so the computer could read them correctly.

Next, we fixed the tables. Instead of using confusing technical codes from the sensors, we renamed everything to simple terms like "wind speed" or "gas levels." After that, we combined two different sets of info—weather and pollution—into one big master list. This was the only way to see exactly how things like rain or wind change the air we breathe.

Finally, we turned all those thousands of numbers into the charts and maps you see on this site. These visuals show you when the air is cleanest during the day, which months are the worst, and even which direction the pollution is blowing from. Basically, we took a huge, boring list of numbers and turned it into a clear story that anyone can understand.
