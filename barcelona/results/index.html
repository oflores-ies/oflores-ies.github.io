Results of pollution in Barcelona (Sants) graphically

Research Visualizations

We created visualizations that show how pollution changes depending on the time of day or the month of the year, heatmaps to detect moments with the highest nitrogen dioxide, and wind analysis to discover which direction the most polluted air comes from. This whole process allowed us to turn a long list of numbers into visual conclusions that are very easy to understand.

Air pollution in Barcelona-Sants (1994-2025)

Nitrogen dioxide evolution in Barcelona−Sants

The first chart shows us the daily and weekly rhythm of pollution in Barcelona-Sants. If you look at the lines, you will notice they always spike at the same times: early morning and late afternoon, matching exactly when people are commuting to and from work. On weekends, especially Sundays, the lines drop significantly because there is much less traffic in the city. The different colors help us track various pollutants, highlighting nitrogen and tiny particles, which are the ones that most affect the air we breathe every day.

The second chart is a heat map that lets us travel through time, from 1998 to 2005, to see how nitrogen dioxide levels have changed. It works like a traffic light: blue colors mean cleaner air, while red and purple tones mark the most polluted moments. By looking at the squares, it is very easy to spot patterns—the early morning hours are always blue because the city is asleep, but bright red spots appear during the day, revealing peak pollution times. This panel is key to understanding which years were the most critical and how air quality shifts with the seasons.
